My research program is structured around the interplay between **non-perturbative gauge dynamics** and **quantum information in quantum field theory**.

## Core Questions

1. How can Bell inequalities and related observables characterize non-local structure in relativistic QFT?
2. Can information-theoretic quantities (relative entropy, entanglement diagnostics) act as probes of confinement and phases of gauge theories?
3. How do modular methods and operator-algebraic structures clarify strongly coupled QFT phenomena?

## Methodological Strategy

- Algebraic and geometric QFT methods (local algebras, modular tools).
- BRST-consistent formulations for gauge sectors.
- Analytical approximations combined with reproducible numerical workflows.
- Cross-fertilization with effective models from topological condensed matter.

## 2026–2029 Goals

- Develop Bell-based criteria applicable to confining gauge sectors.
- Extend modular-entropy methods to broader interacting models.
- Consolidate computational pipelines for QFT information observables.
- Train graduate students in non-perturbative QFT and quantum information.
