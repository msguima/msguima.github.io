## Overview

Relative entropy is a fundamental measure of distinguishability between quantum states. In quantum field theory, the Araki–Uhlmann relative entropy between the vacuum and coherent/excited states can be computed exactly for free fields, and encodes key physical information about entanglement structure and the spectrum of modular Hamiltonians.

## Key Results

- Exact computation of Araki–Uhlmann relative entropy for coherent states in free scalar field theory
- Study of entanglement entropy in Rindler wedges and causal diamonds using the replica trick and modular theory
- Connection between relative entropy and the Bekenstein bound / positivity of energy
- Extension to massive fields (Proca, spin-1) and gauge-fixed formulations
- Analysis of quantum channel capacities and information-theoretic quantities in QFT

## Technical Tools

- Tomita–Takesaki modular theory
- Bisognano–Wichmann theorem
- Replica trick and twist operators
- Gaussian state formalism for free fields
