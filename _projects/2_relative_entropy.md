## Overview

Entanglement entropy and relative entropy are central quantities in quantum information theory. This project studies how these measures manifest in the context of relativistic quantum field theory, where the Hilbert space factorisation assumed in finite-dimensional systems does not straightforwardly hold.

## Key Questions

- How does the relative entropy between the vacuum and an excited (one-particle) state vary with the size and shape of the spatial region considered?
- What is the role of the modular Hamiltonian in capturing quantum correlations?
- Can the Araki–Uhlmann relative entropy be computed explicitly for free fields and used to bound other quantum information quantities?

## Key Results

- Explicit computation of the Araki–Uhlmann relative entropy for free scalar fields between the vacuum and one-particle states in Rindler wedges
- Analysis of the monotonicity and positivity properties of relative entropy in QFT
- Connections to the Bekenstein bound and energy–entropy inequalities
- Extension to Proca (massive vector) fields

## Technical Tools

- Tomita–Takesaki modular theory
- Araki–Uhlmann relative entropy via operator algebraic methods
- Pusz–Woronowicz variational formula
