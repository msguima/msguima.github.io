## Overview

This project investigates quantum non-locality in relativistic quantum field theory by constructing and studying Bell inequalities — originally formulated for finite-dimensional quantum mechanics — in the continuous, infinite-dimensional setting of QFT. The goal is to understand the structure of quantum correlations encoded in the vacuum state of interacting and free field theories.

## Key Results

Using **Weyl operators** localised in causally complete regions (Rindler wedges and double-cone "diamond" regions) and the **Tomita–Takesaki modular theory**:

- Demonstrated maximal and near-maximal violations of the Bell-CHSH inequality for free scalar fields in Minkowski space
- Extended the analysis to Mermin inequalities for multipartite entanglement
- Treated spin-1 (Proca) vector fields and established BRST-invariant formulations for abelian gauge theories
- Developed a numerical platform for computing Bell violations beyond analytic reach
- Studied Bell inequalities with Unruh–DeWitt detectors and in diamond-shaped spacetime regions
- Investigated cat states (superpositions of coherent states) as probes of Bell violations
- Explored Bell inequalities in Gribov-modified gluon propagators

## Technical Tools

- Tomita–Takesaki modular theory and the KMS condition
- Weyl operators and smeared field algebras (test-function methods)
- Numerical diagonalization of modular Hamiltonians
