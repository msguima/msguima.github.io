## Overview

Local observables in quantum field theory are naturally described by **von Neumann algebras of type III₁** — a fact with profound implications for entanglement theory. Unlike type I algebras (finite quantum systems), type III₁ algebras do not admit pure states on subalgebras, making conventional entanglement measures ill-defined.

This project studies the mathematical structures of modular theory and their physical consequences, including **embezzlement of entanglement**, generalized symmetries, and connections to black hole physics.

## Key Themes

- Tomita-Takesaki modular operators and modular flow
- Embezzlement of entanglement using Weyl operators
- Connections between modular theory and generalized (higher-form) symmetries
- Type III₁ algebras and their implications for quantum information in QFT

## Status

This is an ongoing and emerging direction, connected to the broader research program on quantum information in QFT.
