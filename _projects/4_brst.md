## Overview

BRST symmetry is the residual fermionic symmetry left after gauge fixing in quantum field theory. In the presence of Gribov copies, BRST symmetry is softly broken, raising questions about unitarity and the physical state space. This project studies the nature and consequences of this breaking.

## Key Results

- Explicit construction of BRST-invariant composite operators in the Gribov–Zwanziger framework
- Ward identities and their modifications in the refined GZ theory
- BRST-invariant formulation of Bell inequalities for gauge fields (interface with quantum information)
- Renormalisability of the RGZ action to all orders in perturbation theory
- Physical interpretation of the Gribov parameter and its relation to the string tension
