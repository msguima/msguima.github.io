## Overview

Colour confinement — the fact that quarks and gluons are never observed as free particles — remains one of the deepest unsolved problems in theoretical physics. The **Gribov–Zwanziger (GZ) framework** addresses a fundamental problem in the quantisation of non-abelian gauge theories: the existence of multiple gauge copies (Gribov copies) that complicate the path-integral quantisation via the Faddeev–Popov procedure.

## Background

In the Landau gauge, the standard Faddeev–Popov procedure breaks down because there exist multiple field configurations related by gauge transformations that satisfy the gauge-fixing condition. Gribov's original proposal was to restrict the functional integral to the first Gribov region, where the Faddeev–Popov operator is positive definite. Zwanziger implemented this restriction via a local, renormalisable action — the GZ action.

## Key Results

- Study of the GZ propagators and their positivity violations as signals of confinement
- Extension of the GZ framework to curved backgrounds and to the electroweak sector
- Analysis of colour confinement in the Refined Gribov–Zwanziger (RGZ) model, which accounts for dynamical mass generation for gluons
- Computation of gluon and quark propagators in the RGZ model and comparison with lattice QCD data
- Study of Gribov ambiguities in maximal abelian gauge and consequences for the dual superconductor picture of confinement
- Thermodynamic properties of the RGZ model at finite temperature
