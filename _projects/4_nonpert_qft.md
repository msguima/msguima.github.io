## Overview

Beyond perturbation theory, quantum field theories exhibit rich structures: dynamical mass generation, topological sectors, dualities between seemingly different theories, and condensation phenomena. This project encompasses several lines of research on non-perturbative aspects of QFT that are not directly related to the Gribov–Zwanziger framework.

## Research Lines

### Duality and Bosonization in 2+1 Dimensions

- Duality mappings between fermionic and bosonic models in 2+1 dimensions
- Applications to condensed-matter-inspired QFT models
- Topological mass generation and Chern–Simons terms

### Dynamical Mass Generation

- Schwinger–Dyson equations and gap equations in gauge theories
- Cornwall–Jackiw–Tomboulis (CJT) effective potential approach
- Connections to chiral symmetry breaking

### Topological Aspects

- BF theories and their role in confinement
- Vortex condensation and dual superconductivity
