## Overview

This project investigates quantum non-locality in relativistic quantum field theory by constructing and studying Bell inequalities—originally formulated for quantum mechanics—in the continuous, infinite-dimensional setting of QFT.

## Key Results

Using **Weyl operators** localized in causally complete regions (Rindler wedges, double-cone "diamond" regions) and the **Tomita-Takesaki modular theory**, we have:

- Demonstrated maximal and near-maximal violations of the Bell-CHSH inequality for free scalar fields
- Extended the analysis to Mermin inequalities for multi-partite entanglement
- Treated spin-1 (Proca) vector fields and established BRST-invariant formulations for gauge theories
- Developed a numerical platform for computing Bell violations beyond analytic reach
- Studied Bell inequalities with Unruh-DeWitt detectors and in diamond-shaped spacetime regions
- Investigated cat states (superpositions of coherent states) as probes of Bell violations

## Technical Tools

- Tomita-Takesaki modular theory and the KMS condition
- Weyl operators and smeared field algebras (test-function methods)
- Numerical diagonalization of modular Hamiltonians
