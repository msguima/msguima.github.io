## Overview

Gauge theories present unique challenges for the definition and computation of entanglement entropy and other quantum information measures. The presence of local gauge symmetry leads to superselection sectors and edge modes that must be carefully handled. This project studies these issues in abelian and non-abelian gauge theories and their connections to gravity.

## Key Questions

- How does one define entanglement entropy in gauge theories, where the Hilbert space does not factorise across a spatial boundary?
- What is the role of edge modes and boundary degrees of freedom?
- Can quantum information measures (entanglement entropy, mutual information, relative entropy) probe confinement?
- What is the interplay between Gribov copies and quantum entanglement?

## Key Results

- Extension of Bell inequality analysis to gauge theories (abelian and non-abelian)
- Study of entanglement entropy in the presence of Gribov copies and modified propagators
- Analysis of superselection sectors in abelian gauge theories and their effect on Bell violations
- Connection between the Haag duality property and the presence of topological excitations

## Technical Tools

- Algebraic quantum field theory (AQFT) and the Haag–Kastler framework
- Modular theory and Tomita–Takesaki operators
- Jones index and subfactor theory
