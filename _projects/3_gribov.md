## Overview

In non-Abelian gauge theories, fixing the Lorenz (Landau) gauge leaves a residual ambiguity—multiple gauge field configurations (Gribov copies) satisfy the gauge condition. The Gribov–Zwanziger (GZ) framework removes these copies by restricting the path integral to the first Gribov region, producing an infrared-modified gluon propagator and a confining quark–gluon vertex.

## Key Results

- Derivation and study of the Refined Gribov–Zwanziger (RGZ) action including condensates of dimension-2 operators
- Gluon and ghost propagators in the RGZ framework: comparison with lattice QCD data
- Extension of the GZ approach to curved spacetime and finite temperature
- BRST symmetry breaking and its physical consequences in the GZ framework
- Gribov copies in the maximal Abelian gauge and their role in Abelian dominance

## Technical Tools

- Faddeev–Popov quantisation beyond the perturbative regime
- Gribov horizon and the no-pole condition
- Localisation of the horizon function via auxiliary fields
- Operator product expansion and renormalisation group
