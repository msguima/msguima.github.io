## Course Overview

This graduate course introduces the main non-perturbative methods in quantum field theory (QFT), with emphasis on applications to quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and the problem of colour confinement.

**Target audience:** Graduate students (Master's and Doctoral) in Physics.

**Language:** Portuguese (material available in English on request).

## Topics Covered

1. **Review of gauge theories** — Yang-Mills action, BRST symmetry, Faddeev-Popov procedure
2. **The Gribov problem** — gauge-fixing ambiguities, Gribov copies, the Gribov region
3. **Gribov-Zwanziger framework** — horizon condition, propagators, refined GZ action
4. **Dyson-Schwinger equations** — truncation schemes and non-perturbative propagators
5. **Lattice QCD basics** — discretization, Wilson action, Monte Carlo methods, comparison with continuum
6. **Instantons and topological effects** — BPST instanton, 't Hooft vertex, θ-vacuum
7. **Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking** — order parameters, effective models (NJL, quark-meson)
8. **Confinement criteria** — Wilson loop, Polyakov loop, string tension, Gribov-Zwanziger vs. dual superconductor

## Prerequisites

- Quantum Field Theory I (canonical quantization, perturbation theory, Feynman rules)
- Classical Field Theory and gauge invariance
- Statistical Mechanics (useful but not required)
