## Course Overview

This graduate-level course provides an advanced treatment of quantum mechanics and serves as a bridge to quantum field theory. Special attention is given to foundational questions, path integral methods, and modern connections to quantum information theory — areas directly related to current research in the group.

## Topics Covered

- **Foundations of quantum mechanics:** Hilbert space formalism, observables, measurement, density matrices
- **Symmetries:** groups, representations, discrete and continuous symmetries
- **Path integral formulation:** Feynman path integral, stationary phase approximation, connection to statistical mechanics
- **Semiclassical methods:** WKB, instantons, tunnelling
- **Open quantum systems:** master equations, Lindblad formalism, decoherence
- **Entanglement and quantum information:** entanglement entropy, Bell inequalities, quantum correlations
- **Introduction to quantum field theory:** second quantisation, Fock space, free fields, vacuum structure

## Prerequisites

- Undergraduate quantum mechanics
- Classical mechanics and electrodynamics
- Some familiarity with complex analysis
