## Course Overview

This course introduces the field-theoretic formulation of classical physics, building the conceptual and mathematical foundations for quantum field theory. It covers scalar, vector (gauge), and spinor fields at the classical level.

**Target audience:** Advanced undergraduate and beginning graduate students.

**Language:** Portuguese.

## Topics Covered

1. **Lagrangian field theory** — action principle, Euler-Lagrange equations for fields, symmetries
2. **Noether's theorem** — continuous symmetries and conservation laws (energy-momentum tensor, angular momentum, currents)
3. **Scalar field theory** — Klein-Gordon equation, real and complex scalar fields, spontaneous symmetry breaking
4. **Classical electrodynamics as a gauge theory** — Maxwell's equations, gauge invariance, covariant formulation
5. **Non-Abelian gauge theories** — Yang-Mills fields, structure constants, Bianchi identity
6. **The Dirac field** — Dirac equation, Lorentz group representation, bilinears
7. **Higgs mechanism** — local gauge invariance, mass generation, Goldstone theorem
8. **Introduction to the Standard Model** — field content, symmetry group (overview)

## Prerequisites

- Classical Mechanics (Lagrangian/Hamiltonian)
- Classical Electrodynamics (vector potential, Maxwell equations)
- Special Relativity (4-vectors, tensor notation)
- Mathematical Methods in Physics
