## Course Overview

This undergraduate course introduces students to the mechanics of fluids — liquids and gases — from a theoretical physics perspective. It covers both the mathematical formalism and the physical intuition behind fluid behaviour, with an emphasis on problem-solving and connections to other areas of physics.

## Topics Covered

- **Fluid statics:** pressure, buoyancy, Pascal's and Archimedes' principles
- **Fluid kinematics:** streamlines, flow fields, continuity equation
- **Fluid dynamics:** Euler and Navier–Stokes equations, Bernoulli's principle
- **Viscosity and viscous flows:** Poiseuille flow, Reynolds number, laminar vs. turbulent flow
- **Waves in fluids:** sound waves, surface waves, dispersion relations
- **Introduction to turbulence and dimensional analysis**

## Prerequisites

- Classical Mechanics (undergraduate level)
- Calculus and differential equations
