## Course Overview

This graduate course bridges **quantum information theory** and **relativistic quantum field theory (QFT)**. It covers the formalism needed to define and compute entanglement measures in QFT, with applications to black hole thermodynamics, quantum gravity, and the study of phases of matter.

**Target audience:** Graduate students (Master's and Doctoral) in Physics.

**Language:** Portuguese (material available in English on request).

## Topics Covered

1. **Quantum information basics** — density matrices, von Neumann entropy, relative entropy, mutual information
2. **Operator algebras in QFT** — C\*-algebras, von Neumann algebras, types I, II, III
3. **Tomita-Takesaki modular theory** — modular operator, modular Hamiltonian, KMS condition
4. **Entanglement entropy in QFT** — area law, replica trick, entanglement for free fields
5. **Relative entropy in QFT** — Araki-Uhlmann relative entropy, monotonicity, applications
6. **Bell inequalities in QFT** — CHSH inequality, Weyl operators, Cirel'son bound in QFT
7. **Unruh effect and entanglement** — Rindler decomposition, Hawking radiation, thermal density matrices
8. **Holographic entanglement entropy** — Ryu-Takayanagi formula, bulk reconstruction (overview)
9. **Applications** — quantum phase transitions, symmetry-resolved entanglement, recent developments

## Prerequisites

- Quantum Field Theory I
- Quantum Mechanics (including density matrices and basic quantum information)
- Mathematical methods in physics (functional analysis helpful but not required)
