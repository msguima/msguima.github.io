## Ementa / Syllabus

This graduate course introduces quantum information concepts (entanglement, Bell inequalities, relative entropy) in the relativistic setting of quantum field theory.

### Topics

1. Review: quantum information in finite dimensions (qubits, density matrices, entanglement)
2. Bell inequalities: CHSH, Mermin, and their violations
3. Algebraic quantum field theory: local algebras and the Haag–Kastler framework
4. Tomita–Takesaki modular theory
5. The Bisognano–Wichmann theorem
6. Entanglement entropy in QFT: Rindler wedges and causal diamonds
7. Relative entropy: Araki–Uhlmann formula and its applications
8. Bell inequalities in QFT: Weyl operators and maximal violations
9. Unruh effect and quantum information
10. Selected topics: cat states, multi-partite entanglement, gauge fields

## Prerequisites

- Quantum mechanics (advanced)
- Quantum field theory (introductory)
- Basic quantum information (helpful but not required)
